No meter — meterless routes, highest damage first
The Real Punish
~2300 dmg Intermediate No meter Knockdown
cr.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK
Why it works: cr.MP is +6 on hit and Special-cancellable. Tatsu HK has 7f startup — the cancel window connects easily. Tatsu HK hits crouching opponents (most Tatsus don't) and deals 1600 raw damage. Total with scaling lands around 2300. KD+34 for oki. This is the real meterless punish route — much stronger than cr.MP xx Hadouken HP which only deals 700.
TC Ender
~2100 dmg Intermediate No meter Knockdown
st.MP TC st.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK
Why it works: st.MP Target Combos into itself (Viscera Piercer). The 2nd hit of the TC is Special-cancellable — Tatsu HK (7f) connects cleanly. Tatsu HK hits crouchers and gives KD+34. Strong punish off any situation where you land st.MP close. The TC is guaranteed — no link timing required.
Light Confirm → Oki
~1600 dmg Beginner No meter Knockdown
cr.LP → cr.LP xx cancel Tatsu MK
Why it works: cr.LP +5 on hit links into second cr.LP (1f window). Cancel into Tatsu MK (11f startup). Tatsu MK gives KD+41 — the best meterless oki advantage you can get off lights. Note: Tatsu MK whiffs on crouchers at certain spacings, so confirm range matters. Your go-to light confirm if you want oki over raw damage.
The Backbone
~1200 dmg Beginner No meter
cr.MK xx cancel Hadouken MP
Why it works: cr.MK +1 on hit, Special cancel into Hadouken MP (14f). This is your neutral game — not your highest damage option but your most accessible one. Safe at range, confirms your footsie poke into damage. The foundation everything else builds from.
Drive meter — DR and OD routes, highest damage first
DR Punish Route
~2900 dmg Intermediate 1 bar DR Knockdown
cr.MK DR cr.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK
Why it works: cr.MK is only +1 on hit — normally cannot link into anything. Drive Rush cancel adds +4 frame advantage, making it effectively +5. cr.MP has 6f startup: 5-6 = -1 normally, but with DR it's +5-6 = a viable link. Then cr.MP xx Tatsu HK for the big ender. This is the textbook DR use — spending 1 bar to convert a poke you couldn't otherwise extend into a 2900 damage knockdown.
OD Hadouken Oki
~1500 dmg Beginner 1 bar OD Knockdown
cr.MK xx cancel Hadouken OD
Why it works: OD Hadouken gives KD+55 and is +2 even on block. Lower damage than the DR route but simpler execution — no link required. The unique value is fullscreen knockdown. You can be at max range, land cr.MK, spend 1 bar, and still get oki. Use this when you need the knockdown more than you need the damage.
Super meter — super cancel routes, highest damage first
The Nuke
~5200 dmg Intermediate 3 bars super Knockdown
cr.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK xx super cancel L3 Sip
Why it works: cr.MP xx Tatsu HK is your strongest normal → special route. Tatsu HK is Super-cancellable — input QCFx2+K during the Tatsu animation. L3 Sip (8f) connects in juggle state for the full 4000 hit. Total lands around 5200 with scaling. This is Gouki's highest accessible damage off a single normal confirm. Save L3 for when this will close out a round.
Light Confirm Super
~4500 dmg Intermediate 3 bars super Knockdown
cr.LP → cr.LP xx cancel Tatsu MK xx super cancel L3 Sip
Why it works: cr.LP confirm into Tatsu MK, then super cancel into L3. Tatsu MK is Super-cancellable so input QCFx2+K during the spin. Slightly lower damage than The Nuke due to light scaling, but the confirm is off your safest opener (cr.LP is -1 on block). This is what you throw when you land the light confirm and see your opponent's HP is in range.
Empyrean Punish
~4200 dmg Intermediate 2 bars super Knockdown
cr.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK xx super cancel L2 Empyrean
Why it works: Same route as The Nuke but using L2 instead of L3. L2 Empyrean gives KD+29~97 (corner gives massive oki). Saves a super bar while still dealing strong damage. Preferred over L3 when you're not in kill range but want the corner oki advantage. L2 is also fully invincible (1–9) so can be used as a reversal → combo.
DR Max Punish
~5800 dmg Advanced 1 DR + 3 super Knockdown
cr.MK DR cr.MP xx cancel Tatsu HK xx super cancel L3 Sip
Why it works: The full resource dump. DR unlocks cr.MP after cr.MK (+4 advantage bridges the gap), then cr.MP xx Tatsu HK xx L3 Sip for maximum output. Around 5800 damage off a footsie poke. This is the combo you land when you see a punishable move at mid-range and have the meter ready. Requires tight DR timing — drill the cr.MK DR cr.MP link separately before adding the ender.
Oki loops — position + frame math
The Tatsu MK Loop
~1620 dmg Intermediate No meter Knockdown
st.MK xx cancel Tatsu MK → fwd dash → fwd dash → meaty st.MP
Why st.MK first: Tatsu MK whiffs on crouching opponents. st.MK forces crouchers to stand, so Tatsu MK reliably connects regardless of opponent stance.

The frame math (Zephyr's double dash setup):
Tatsu MK gives KD+41. Gouki's forward dash = 19f. Two dashes = 38f. 41 − 38 = +3 remaining.
st.MP has 6f startup. 6 − 3 = 3f gap — the fastest move in the game is 4f, so st.MP lands as a guaranteed meaty.

Why it matters: This brings you back to exactly the same +1 pressure scenario you started from but now in or near the corner. The loop repeats: meaty st.MP → continue pressure → knockdown → double dash → meaty st.MP. Low damage per cycle but relentless positioning advantage.
Meaty setups — frame math reference
Forward throw → meaty cr.MK
KD+28 Beginner
Fwd throw KD+28 walk forward → meaty cr.MK
Math: KD+28. cr.MK has 8f startup and 3 active frames — wide enough window to walk forward and still time the meaty comfortably. cr.MK on meaty is effectively +3 hit / -4 block from the best active frame. Your primary wakeup tool after forward throw.
Tatsu MK → meaty cr.MK (no dash)
KD+41 Beginner
Tatsu MK KD+41 walk forward → meaty cr.MK
Math: KD+41 is the largest meterless knockdown advantage you can get from a light confirm. Walk forward freely — you have 41 frames minus however long your walk takes. cr.MK's 3 active frames give you wiggle room on timing. On meaty hit you're +3 into cr.MK xx Hadouken for free damage.
Tatsu MK → double dash → meaty st.MP
KD+41 Intermediate Corner loop
Tatsu MK KD+41 dash + dash = 38f meaty st.MP
Math: KD+41 − (19f + 19f) = +3. st.MP is 6f startup. 6 − 3 = 3f gap — beats the fastest move in the game (4f). Guaranteed meaty regardless of wakeup timing.

Why double dash over walk: The dashes cover ground faster than walking, putting you in the correct range for st.MP. Walking to the same spot takes too many frames and burns your +3 window. This setup is the foundation of Gouki's pressure loop.
L2 Empyrean → meaty (corner)
KD+97 corner Easy — huge window
L2 Empyrean KD+97 corner walk or dash → meaty anything
Math: KD+97 in corner gives you 97 frames — nearly 1.5 seconds of wakeup time. You can walk forward, jump, fake a crossup, set up an ambiguous meaty, or simply stand and wait. This is the biggest oki swing in Gouki's kit and why L2 Empyrean is often preferred over L3 Sip when you're not in kill range.
